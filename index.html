Rules Reference

Standard blackjack concepts with table-rule differences labeled instead of hidden.

Goal

Beat the dealer by ending closer to 21 without going over. You compete against the dealer, not other players.

Card Values

Number cards use pip value. J, Q, and K count as 10. An ace counts as 1 or 11, whichever helps without busting.

Deal

Players get two face-up cards. The dealer usually shows one upcard and keeps one hole card face down.

Blackjack

A first-two-card ace plus a 10-value card is blackjack. A 3:2 payout is the benchmark to look for.

Hit

Take another card. If your total goes over 21 after ace adjustment, you bust and lose the hand.

Stand

Keep your current total and end your action. This is legal on any active, unbusted hand.

Double

Double the bet, take exactly one card, then stand. This app allows it only on the first two cards.

Split

If your first two cards have the same blackjack value, split them into two hands with a second equal bet.

Dealer Turn

After players finish, the dealer reveals the hole card and draws by table rule. This practice table uses H17.

Push

If your final total ties the dealer, the hand pushes and the original bet is returned.

Insurance

Insurance is a side bet offered only when the dealer shows an ace. Beginner strategy says skip it.

Variants

H17/S17, DAS, surrender, resplitting aces, and 6:5 payouts are house rules. Read the felt before playing.
